Who we are

The father of size-reduction technology.

Microtec GmbH is the gold standard in micronization and ultra-fine powder production. Its founder, Josef Fischer — known as the "father of size-reduction technology" — has pioneered some of the world's most challenging particle applications, from HP inkjet and laser-jet toner to Stealth Bomber reflective coatings and time-release pharmaceutical particle control.

Today, Microtec Development & Holdings — a U.S.-based company that has acquired 100% of Microtec GmbH's business assets and patents — is transitioning from equipment sales to a platform built on technology licensing, operations, and waste-to-value.

The Process

From feed to finished powder.

A four-stage system — feed, mill, classify, treat — that reduces complex materials to exact micron-level specifications in cryogenic, ambient, or thermodynamic configurations.

The signature process

Material in. Micronized powder out.

A wide range of feedstocks enter the UTM Turbo Mill, where shear, attrition, compression, and impact forces drive particles down to the micron level — engineered fineness across many materials, not just size reduction.

Stage 01 · Feed

LN2 Screw/Auger

The micronization process begins with material entering a motorized screw/auger. In an ambient configuration, hot oil circulates within the shaft, which helps dry the material and releases moisture.

Stage 02 · Mill

UTM Turbo Mill

Material is fed to the UTM Turbo Mill, which can operate in a cryogenic or ambient setting. The desired particle size and moisture release can be achieved by adjusting the shape and material of the grinding elements and the mill's speed and temperature.

Stage 03 · Classify

Air Classifier

Unclassified material is exposed to centrifugal forces inside the classifier wheel and vacuum forces outside it. By adjusting the classifier wheel's RPM, blade count, spacing, configuration, and vacuum pressure, precise particle classification and separation are achieved.

Stage 04 · Treat

The BioChar Cyclone

In a heating configuration, the BioChar Cyclone suspends smaller material in a cyclonic centrifugal state and heat-treats it at 250–320 degrees Celsius. The treated product exits from the bottom, while water and hot air exit from the top.

Construction

Cementitious Materials

Micronization turns industrial byproducts and minerals into reactive supplementary cementitious materials (SCMs) that replace a share of Portland cement — cutting embodied carbon while improving concrete strength and durability. Precise control of particle size and surface area is what makes these powders pozzolanically active and consistent from batch to batch.

MaterialsSCMs, concrete additives, blended cements, grouts, mortars, and more.
Industrial minerals

Minerals & Aggregates

Ground and air-classified industrial minerals are the workhorses of paints, plastics, paper, coatings, and construction. Microtec engineers exact particle-size distributions and morphology so each mineral delivers the brightness, opacity, flow, or reinforcement its end use demands.

MaterialsCalcium carbonate, kaolin, clay, mica, talcum powder, wollastonite, silica.
Energy storage

Battery Materials

Battery performance lives and dies on particle engineering — cathode, anode, and precursor powders must hit precise size, shape, and surface area to govern energy density, charge rate, and cycle life. Our milling and classification produce the ultra-fine, tightly distributed powders modern cells require.

MaterialsCathode, anode, LFP, NMC, graphite, lithium compounds, precursors.
Solar

Solar Panels

From silicon and polysilicon to solar glass and recovered wafer kerf, PV manufacturing depends on clean, precisely sized feedstocks. Micronization reclaims high-purity material from production streams and re-engineers it to spec — improving yield and cutting waste.

MaterialsSilicon, polysilicon, wafer kerf, solar glass, encapsulant materials.
Metals

Metals & Alloys

Metal powders drive additive manufacturing, coatings, and recycling — and each process needs a specific particle size, shape, and flowability. Microtec processes aluminum, copper, nickel, cobalt, and rare-earth streams, including recycled feed, into engineered powders for high-value reuse.

MaterialsAluminum, copper, nickel, cobalt, rare earths, recycling streams.
Critical minerals

Critical Minerals & Rare Earths

Critical minerals are strategically scarce and notoriously hard to process. Our cryogenic and ambient milling liberates and refines rare-earth and critical-mineral feedstocks into consistent, high-purity powders — strengthening domestic supply chains for magnets, electronics, and defense.

MaterialsNeodymium, dysprosium, lanthanum, scandium, graphite, niobium, tantalum.
Food

Food Ingredients

Texture, dissolution, and mouthfeel all trace back to particle size. Microtec micronizes flours, proteins, spices, and functional powders — often cryogenically, to protect flavor and heat-sensitive nutrients — for uniform blending and consistent performance.

MaterialsFlours, proteins, spices, sugars, salts, starches, functional powders.

AggrePlex

Low-Carbon Cementitious Materials

AggrePlex is Microtec's flagship commercialization platform for producing high-performance ground-glass pozzolan. Using Microtec's advanced micronization and classification technology, AggrePlex transforms recovered glass into PozzoDyne™, an activated supplementary cementitious material designed to partially replace ordinary Portland cement in concrete.

This case study demonstrates Microtec's ability to move from technology to full-scale industrial deployment — combining feedstock strategy, precision processing, product validation, and commercial offtake development.

Lehigh Technologies

Proven Commercial Scale in Micronized Rubber

Lehigh Technologies, a subsidiary of Michelin, represents a leading example of Microtec's technology operating successfully at full industrial scale. By integrating Microtec's advanced milling systems, Lehigh converts end-of-life tire rubber into highly engineered micronized rubber powders used across tire and industrial applications.

This case study demonstrates Microtec's ability to deliver long-term reliability, consistent performance, and scalable material transformation in one of the most demanding manufacturing environments.
